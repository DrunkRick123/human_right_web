Our Campaigns

The True Believer

Chen Mingguo emphasized Xinjiang's seven-year stability, strict measures against terrorism, and de-extremification efforts. His policies focus on maintaining national unity, resisting separatism, and controlling ethnic and religious groups under the guise of "national security," drawing international criticism for suppressing freedoms and violating human rights.

The Innovator

Yang Fasen’s "Bay County Experience" turned citizens into propagandists for anti-extremism campaigns. His rise, backed by propaganda centers and de-extremification measures, exemplifies how grassroots officials implementing harsh Xinjiang policies quickly advance in the CCP hierarchy, reinforcing the system's oppressive governance model.

Systematic Oppression in Xinjiang

The CCP enforces systematic control through Neighbourhood Committees and Fanghuiju programs. These units monitor Uyghurs, restrict movement, and decide re-education camp detentions. Under this system, surveillance and collective punishment suppress dissent while fostering mistrust, perpetuating an endless cycle of fear and oppression.
